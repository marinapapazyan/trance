Editors' and Designers' Notes


Nerves.
Connecting my brain to my spine.
 
Damaged.
A nerve injury.
This notion of pain,
of broken that cracks the shape of
your vision.
 
Slightly tilted.
Soft edges.
Unbalanced.
 
Yet, regeneration.
Electrical stimuli
reversing a nerve injury.
Somehow a broken that can
be unbroken.
 
—Pauline Bauvin
 
 
You could call History a
nervous episode.
Balls of nerves rolling around,
bumping into each other.
Pleasing and hurting each other.
Breaking and calming each other’s
nerves.
Fixing wrong fixes and wronging
past rights.
Ruling over their nerves which rule
over them.
An endless beginningless circle of
insulting and apologising.
A feedback loop of input output
that some would cheaply call,
a fuck.
 
—Yusuf Zucchero
 
 
I was nervous.
Writing your name with a shaky hand,
yet so controlled and
curated.
A fake parkinson’s symptom.
A staged cocaine overdose.
“Just for the sake of aesthetics?”
“Yes. A well made visual strategy, a
design solution.”
Sitting still, I was just about to
finish the fifth A4 paper with
wobbly handwriting, right before these
wet black worms started to crawl. My
hand trembled still but out of control.
Ink splashed. My nib bit the paper. Wet
names started to pronounce themselves.
“I have never understood my nerves.”
Epson scanner, heigh-resolution scanning
with 2400 dpi. Back to the screen
to the indesign file, I escaped from being
nerved by the biotic nonexistence.
Sitting still, I had only to wait for the
.tiff file to be saved on my desktop.
 
—Kexin Hao
